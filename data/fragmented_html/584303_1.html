The most important wax classes in barley are the alkanes, esters, aldehydes, primary alcohols, free fatty acids, beta-diketones and hydroxy-beta-diketones. The latter two lipids are absent on leaf blades and awns. Waxes on these two organs can be distinguished in Foma, however, by the presence of secondary alcohols in the awn but not the leaf blade wax.
