Possessing chewing mouth parts, they cause minute damage to seeds that the opened cones may still retain, although investigations in the Castro Tree indicate that few seeds are really harmed, those still attached to the opened cones maintaining as much as 75% viability. This may result from strong vascular connections between the seeds and cone scales.
