The sequoia community, much like other forest communities, is stratified, or layered. The uppermost layer is the vegetation of the canopy, comprising the crowns of the giant sequoia and of other tree species such as the white fir (Abies concolor), sugar pine (Pinus lambertiana), ponderosa pine (Pinus ponderosa), and often, at lower elevations, the incense-cedar (Libocedrus decurrens).
