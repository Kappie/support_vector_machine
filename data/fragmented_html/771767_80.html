The water quality parameters of primary concern are dissolved oxygen, ammonia, nitrite, carbon dioxide, and gas supersaturation from either oxygen or nitrogen. Dissolved oxygen should not be less than 5 ppm, preferable near saturation, but oxygen supersaturation should be avoided. Recent observations suggest that walleye fry are especially sensitive to supersaturation.
