Brown, dried-out cones remaining upon the tree also harbor a small community of insects between the cone scales. Common in this community is a small lygaeid bug (Ischnorrhynchus resedae), about an eighth-inch long, that hides in the cone's openings when not feeding upon the juices of seeds and nearby foliage.
