How can cenospheres in fly ash be used?

Fly ash contains tiny, hollow, particulate ceramic spheres, typically ranging in diameter from 5 to 75 micrometers, which are called cenospheres (fig. 1). They exhibit some unique properties, such as high energy absorption, which results in protection against electromagnetic interference.
