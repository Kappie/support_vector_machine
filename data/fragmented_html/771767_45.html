Turbid water does an exceptional job of dispersing the fry. In tanks with turbid water, fry are concentrated near the surface, but dispersed horizontally, in clear water, fry have a greater vertical distribution, even going to the bottom of tanks with black lateral walls but gray-colored bottom. The horizontal dispersal of fry in turbid water seems to be the consequence of the light-scattering effect of turbidity which reduces the amount of light reflected
