The small core of the star (typically a sphere of about 10 km in radius) may remain behind as an ultradense "compact object", generally a neutron star, but sometimes, in extreme cases, a black hole. In the case of the Crab Nebula, we can see both the nebula and the neutron star. The neutron star spins very rapidly, about 30 times per second.
