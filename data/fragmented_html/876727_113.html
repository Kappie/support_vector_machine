In contrast with the major strike-slip fault zones, seismicity on the faults of the Santa Clara Valley thrust belt shows strong dependence on changes in normal stress. The static reduction in normal stress (unclamping) that occurred after Loma Prieta on many of these faults was followed by a closely correlated increase in seismicity.
