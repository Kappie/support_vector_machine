Soil carbon on plantings of sweetgum without a cover crop decreased from 2.95 kg/m2 to 2.7 kg/m2 over the first five years of the study. In addition to increasing soil carbon storage, researchers have identified decreases in the bulk density of the soil with conversion from traditional tillage to no-till and biomass crop production.
