Meiotic recombination is initiated by the introduction of programmed double stranded breaks (DSBs) early in meiosis. A commonly observed feature of meiotic recombination is the highly non-uniform distribution of meiotic crossovers in the genome. It is still not clear, however, why crossovers are preferentially localized in hotspots instead of being uniformly distributed.
