osol measurements are complex and challenging. Until recently aerosols studies have been focused on classifying aerosols according to their size distribution. Only recently has the need been recognized, and the likelihood promising, to go one step further and ask what is their composition. Clearly if aerosol loading is to be reduced it is necessary to know what they are made of. Moreover, as more is learned about the health effects undoubtedly some components will be found to be more harmful than others
