Some believe that the pulverized bark, rich in tannin, repels such common ectoparasites as lice and fleas. Other birds, such as flycatchers, will utilize these holes and limbs as nesting sites. The giant sequoia, however, is never exclusively sought out for food or nesting. The vertebrate that comes closest to reliance upon the giant sequoia is still the chickaree.
