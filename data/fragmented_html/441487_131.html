The fewer trees of the upper crown canopy that are destroyed, the poorer the opportunity for shade-intolerant plants to reestablish themselves. With the disappearance of thick accumulations of leaf litter, small-seeded annuals increase greatly, but the shade-intolerant herbs such as the orchid (Goodyera oblongifolia) continue to maintain their populations at about the same levels as before.
