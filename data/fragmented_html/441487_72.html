The literature often states that insects have never been known to kill a mature giant sequoia. Although serious studies are lacking, this assumption may well be true. Actual observation suggests that insect depredations do not seriously affect sequoias after the second year of the seedling stage, and it is unlikely that insect attack alone causes their death.
