Ceanothus not only seems well adapted to fire conditions, but it and lupine, which is a legume, have mutual relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria whose activities enrich the soil with nitrogen. Here, then, is a predetermined system of fertilizing the tree seedlings and saplings which follow in the succession. It is during this juvenile state that nitrogen is most necessary for their successful growth.
