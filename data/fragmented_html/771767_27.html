First feeding walleye (8.5-9.0 mm) have a mouth width of 0.7 mm and a gape of 1.5 mm, which is sufficiently large to cannibalize similar-sized siblings or to consume the standard size starter feed.  Balon (1984) considered the transition of the embryo (prolarva) to exogenous feeding to be the decisive event in development, because failure to accomplish first feeding results in mortality.
