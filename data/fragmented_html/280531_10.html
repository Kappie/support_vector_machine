To reduce transpiration, direct cooling air flow, shade themselves, and/or reflect hot sunlight, they often have few leaves, or have spines, thorns, or hairs instead; in some cases they use only their green “skin” to perform photosynthesis. Some xerophytes have leaves that are tough and waxy or coated with shiny oils, which also cuts down on transpiration, or they have small, fluttery leaves that help to cool the plants.
