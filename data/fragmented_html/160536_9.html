The lake was far enough from the foot of the volcano to be an effective monitor of deep-seated changes under the volcano. The lake was oriented with its long direction radial to the volcano, and quick calculations suggested that tilts on the order of 2 urad would be detectable with reasonably careful measurement procedures.
