Competition within the plant world is strongly, but not exclusively, governed by the sunlight available at the photosynthetic or leaf surfaces. This is partly a function of stature, with the taller plants succeeding, and partly one of chlorophyll density within the leaves, as some shorter plants can compete successfully in reduced light.
