> In interpreting the statutory term "torture," the BIA previously has held that a Haitian national failed to meet his burden of proof by simply adducing anecdotal evidence of "isolated acts of torture" in Haiti's detention facilities, such as burning with cigarettes and electric shock, and no record evidence was adduced that Haiti used torture pervasively or as a matter of policy on detainees.
