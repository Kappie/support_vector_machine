According to 2005 estimates, Angola has 100,000 fixed lines and over 1,300,000 cellular phone users.  Angola’s two main cellular phone operators, Unitel and Movicel now provide service to all provincial capitals.  Over the past year, users have doubled. Rapid growth in the number of cellular phones has strained the system, making both domestic and international dialing difficult.  Most corporate offices and tourist class hotels have telephones and faxes.  I
