Unlike other species, populations of the wild sheep that may have been the ancestors of modern breeds still exist. Initially, certain authors such as Zeuner (1963) had considered the urial (O. vignei) as strongly contributing to Middle East and European sheep after its domestication in the Aralo-Caspian basin.
