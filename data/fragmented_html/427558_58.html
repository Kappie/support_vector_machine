In 1886 the NCCC Committee on Charity organization suggested four categories and some rough statistics: those worthy of permanent relief (9% of all COS applicants); worthy of temporary relief because of sickness, death, accident, and such causes (25%); in need of work rather than relief (40%); and unworthy of any relief (26%).
