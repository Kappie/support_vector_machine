Nordhaus (1992) imposed carbon tax scenarios ranging from a few dollars per ton to hundreds of dollars per ton -- the latter which would effectively eliminate coal from the world economy. He showed that, in the context of his model and its assumptions, that these carbon emission fees would cost the world economy anywhere from less than 1 percent annual loss in Gross National Product to a several percent loss by the year 2100.
