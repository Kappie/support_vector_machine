Perrot (2000) states that the spread of the Neolithic process found its strength in the fusion of two complementary food production strategies developed independently, cereal growing and small livestock rearing, centred respectively on the Levant and the Taurus–Zagros zone, which merged during the second half of the eighth millennium BC.
