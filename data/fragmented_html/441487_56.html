Establishing a territory is an important phenomenon which among others provides its owner enough food upon which to live, but loses much of its survival value if the animals must kill one another to obtain and then protect their defended territories. Hence, the chickaree has evolved a behavior that relies on threats, reminders, and challenges in vocal form.
